Family Member Eval

They liked the overall layout of the website. They really liked the color scheme and pictures. I did not know that the family member was going to read the entire website word for word, but they did. They gave me the following suggestions:

  1. Home Page- Try to have the first picture and wording around the same size.
  2. Home Page- The section with the three columns would look better if they were all even and the pictures were all the same size
  3. FAQs Page- One of the first questions should state "the" instead of "a" because all the rest of the questions referenced "the" Dragon instead of "a" Dragon

One thing that they really liked was the 3D Carousel that I did on the history page. They stated that it was nice. They also stated that it might pose a little bit of a challenge for someone if they are not tech savvy enough to know to keep the cursor on the picture to hold it in place to read the information, but other than that, they were really impressed with me designing my first website.

Progressive Enhancement Writing

I found an article about progressive enhancement that was interesting. From what I read about progressive enhancement; it is being able to provide the user a good experience according to browser preferability. When someone wants a website, I am sure they do not think about all the extra things that need to be considered when designing a website. Anyone who visits the website should be able to access the content desired without problems. The main goal is to access the content in order to take an action, make an informed decision, or just find out information.

Progressive enhancement is creating an experience. It starts with the content markup in HTML, dress up the content with CSS, and interaction functionality with JavaScript. Be careful not to overdo it or go for the gusto with all the enhancements because you could do more harm than good. When someone visits a website they visit for a reason and by creating too many distractions could cause the user to leave the site and search for another site for what they were searching for.

Some of the reasons to focus on progressive enhancement before building your website include a strong foundation, stability, and SEO and accessibility. The article mentioned Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) being built with progressive enhancement as a core principle and they provide good user experiences and are easily accessible.
